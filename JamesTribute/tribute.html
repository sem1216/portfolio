Liza Soberano: A Rising Star in the Philippine Entertainment Industry.

Early Career

Liza Soberano's first television appearance was in the TV series "Wansapanataym" in 2011, where she played a minor role. She also appeared in the teen drama series "Forevermore" in 2014, where she starred opposite Enrique Gil. The series was a massive hit and made Soberano a household name in the Philippines.

Breakthrough Roles

Soberano's breakthrough role came in the romantic drama series "Dolce Amore" in 2016, where she played the lead role of Serena Marchesa. The series was a huge success and earned her critical acclaim for her performance. Soberano also received nominations for Best Actress at various award ceremonies.

In 2017, Soberano starred in the hit film "My Ex and Whys" opposite Enrique Gil. The film was a commercial success and became one of the highest-grossing Filipino films of all time. Soberano's performance in the film was praised, and she received her first Best Actress nomination at the Gawad Urian Awards.

Other Achievements

Aside from her acting career, Soberano is also a successful model. She has endorsed various local and international brands, including Bench, Maybelline, and Cadbury Dairy Milk. In 2018, she was named as one of the most beautiful faces in the world by the TC Candler Independent Critics.

Personal Life

Soberano is known for her beauty, grace, and talent. She is also known for her charitable works and advocacies. In 2016, she launched a fundraising campaign to help children with cleft palate and cleft lip. She is also an advocate for children's rights and education.
